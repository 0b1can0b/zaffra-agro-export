Gujarat's Finest Orchards,
Delivered to the World

India is among the world's largest producers of tropical and subtropical fruits, and Gujarat's orchards represent some of the finest in the nation. From the legendary Alphonso belts of Valsad and Surat to the sun-drenched mango groves of Kutch, Zaffra Agro sources directly from growers whose families have cultivated these varietals for generations.

Our fresh fruit program spans over 120 contracted farming families across Gujarat and the adjacent Konkan belt, covering more than 3,400 acres under active supply chain management. Every consignment is harvested at precisely measured maturity indices, field-sorted for grade, pre-cooled within four hours of picking, and dispatched via temperature-controlled reefer logistics to the port of Mundra or JNPT.

Whether you are a supermarket chain in the UK, a food-service distributor in the UAE, or a specialty importer in Singapore, our B2B supply terms are built around your requirements — from custom packaging and private label to phytosanitary compliance and MRL reporting.

Our Varieties

Six Premium Varieties,
Export-Ready Year-Round

Each variety is selected for its export suitability — superior shelf life, consistent grade, and compliance with importing-country pesticide residue limits.

Alphonso Mango

The undisputed king of mangoes. Sourced from the GI-tagged Devgad and Ratnagiri belts, Alphonso presents a deep saffron-gold flesh, non-fibrous texture, and an intoxicating floral aroma that is unmistakable on the supermarket floor. Brix levels consistently exceed 19°, with acidity well below 0.35%. Available in 3 kg gift packs through to 5 MT palletised bulk for food-service.

Brix 19+ GI Tagged Mar – Jun 3 kg / 5 kg Box

Kesar Mango

Grown in the mineral-rich volcanic soils around Gir in Junagadh district, Kesar is Gujarat's pride and one of India's most exported mango varieties. Its deep orange pulp, low fibre content, and robust sweetness make it ideal for fresh consumption, pulp processing, and premium gift packing. Cold-chain integrity is maintained from orchard through 5°C reefer containers at port.

Brix 18–21 Gujarat GI Apr – Jun 7 kg Corrugated Box

Thompson Seedless Grapes

Sourced from the Anand and Navsari belts of central Gujarat, Thompson Seedless offers elongated berries with translucent green skin, a crisp bite, and balanced sugar-acid ratio. Our grapes comply with EU MRL requirements and are packed in 4.5 kg punnets with SO₂ sachets for extended shelf life during sea-freight. A GlobalG.A.P. audit trail is available on request.

Brix 16–18 EU MRL Compliant Jan – Mar Oct – Nov

Pomegranate — Bhagwa

Bhagwa is India's premium export pomegranate, distinguished by its thin leathery rind, vibrant crimson arils, and low astringency. Grown in Maharashtra's Nashik belt under GlobalG.A.P. supervision, our program guarantees uniform sizing (300–500 g per fruit), brilliant colour, and a shelf life of 10–12 weeks under controlled atmosphere. A favourite with European supermarkets and Gulf hypermarkets alike.

Deep Red Arils GlobalG.A.P. Oct – Feb 300–500g Grade

Banana — Cavendish

Our Cavendish bananas are sourced from certified plantations in Gujarat's Anand and Bharuch districts, harvested at green stage 2 maturity for sea-freight. Each 18.5 kg carton is packed with ethylene-absorbing sachets, allowing controlled ripening at destination. Consistent 14+ finger count, 20–22 cm length, and freedom from bruising or latex staining are standard across all consignments.

Year-Round Stage 2 Harvest 18.5 kg Carton Cold Chain

Chikoo — Sapodilla

Gujarat's Gholvad belt produces India's finest Chikoo, celebrated for its malty, caramel sweetness and smooth, grainy texture. Rich in dietary fibre and natural sugars, Chikoo is gaining significant traction in Asian-diaspora markets across the UK, Canada, and Southeast Asia. We export mature-green Chikoo in 5 kg ventilated corrugated cartons, cold-stored at 12–15°C to inhibit premature softening.

Brix 20+ Gujarat Origin Oct – Feb 5 kg Carton

Our Process

From Orchard Floor to
Your Warehouse

A rigorous four-stage chain of custody guarantees that the quality we harvest in Gujarat arrives intact at your destination port.

01

Harvest

Fruits are hand-harvested by trained teams at precise maturity indices — Brix, firmness, and colour — using non-bruising techniques. Harvest schedules are planned 48 hours in advance to align with container bookings and vessel ETDs.

02

Quality Sort

Every fruit passes through a multi-stage grading line: weight-sizing, colour grading, surface-defect inspection, and Brix spot-testing. Only export-grade produce is packed; the remainder is redirected to domestic premium markets or processing.

03

Pack & Pre-cool

Graded produce is packed into buyer-specified cartons, stickered, and bar-coded, then placed immediately in forced-air pre-coolers. Pulp temperature reaches target within 3–4 hours, halting respiration and extending commercial shelf life by 30–40%.

04

Cold Chain

Pre-cooled cartons are loaded into temperature-monitored reefer containers at the pack-house — never broken until the destination cold store. Real-time data loggers track temperature throughout sea transit, with logs shared digitally to buyers on arrival.
