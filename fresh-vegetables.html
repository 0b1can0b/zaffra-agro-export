Gujarat's Fertile Soils,
Powering Global Kitchens

Gujarat is one of India's foremost vegetable-growing states — home to the largest red-onion exporting clusters in the country, vast stretches of okra and bitter gourd under protective cultivation, and increasingly, high-value crops such as drumstick (moringa) destined for functional food and nutraceutical markets. Zaffra Agro's fresh vegetable programme is built on two decades of direct field relationships and a logistical infrastructure designed for exactitude.

Our contracted vegetable farms are located within 150 km of our Ahmedabad pack-house, enabling same-day field-to-pack-house turnaround across all categories. Every shipment departs with a full phytosanitary dossier: NPPO-issued Phytosanitary Certificate, Certificate of Origin, MRL test reports from NABL-accredited labs, and pre-shipment quality inspection reports.

With year-round supply of key lines such as onions and a tightly managed seasonal programme for okra, bitter gourd, and chilli, Zaffra Agro is the reliable partner for importers, wholesalers, and ethnic retailers who demand consistency of quality and schedule.

01

Alluvial Riverine Soils

Nourished by the Sabarmati and Mahi river basins, our growing regions boast deep, highly fertile alluvial soils. This nutrient-dense terroir is the foundation of our vegetable program — yielding exceptional dry matter content, robust pungency, and the structural integrity required to endure long sea-freight transit without compromising quality.

02

Semi-Arid Climate Advantage

Gujarat’s unique semi-arid microclimate provides the ultimate natural curing environment. Brilliant, sun-drenched winters reduce innate moisture levels in our onions and chillies — naturally extending shelf life, inhibiting decay, and eliminating the need for synthetic post-harvest treatments.

03

Integrated Farm Management

Quality cannot be inspected into a crop; it must be grown into it. Our in-house agronomists partner directly with contracted growers, governing every variable from seed selection to input optimization. This relentless oversight guarantees that every harvest perfectly aligns with stringent international residue tolerances long before it reaches our pack-houses.

Our Varieties

Six Key Vegetables,
Sourced & Shipped with Precision

From high-volume commodity lines to specialist ethnic-market vegetables, our programme is built around the SKUs that matter most to global importers.

Red Onion

Gujarat's red onions are among India's most-exported agricultural commodities, renowned for their sharp pungency, deep crimson skins, and impressive dry matter content — typically 13–15% — that supports long sea-freight journeys of 20–28 days. Our Nasik-type and Bhima Kali varieties are graded to 45–55 mm and 55–65 mm, packed in 20 kg or 25 kg open-mesh jute or polypropylene net sacks, and shipped year-round from Mundra port.

DM 13–15% Year-Round 20 / 25 kg Nets 45–65 mm Grade

White Onion

White onions are preferred by Mexican, American, and Middle Eastern food-service buyers for their milder, sweeter flavour and crisp texture when raw. Gujarat's Talaja and Mahuva belts produce white onions with thin papery skins, bright snow-white flesh, and excellent table presentation. We export in 20 kg net bags with double-layer kraft lining for moisture management, primarily to the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and East Africa.

Mild & Sweet UAE / Gulf Market Oct – Mar 20 kg Net Bag

Okra — Lady's Finger

Okra is one of India's most significant vegetable export earners, with the UK, UAE, and Canada representing key destination markets driven by South Asian and African diaspora demand. Our okra is harvested at 6–8 cm pod length for tenderness, field-graded for freedom from yellow tips and blemishes, pre-cooled to 7–10°C, and dispatched air-freight or sea-cooled within 24 hours of harvest. Shelf life is 10–14 days under 7–10°C storage.

6–8 cm Length Air & Sea Freight Mar – Nov 3 / 5 kg Carton

Bitter Gourd — Karela

Bitter gourd is a staple in South Asian and Southeast Asian cooking, and a rising star in functional food markets given its clinically-studied benefits for blood sugar management. We export two types: the small, intensely-ridged Indian karela (preferred in UK, Canada, Fiji) and the larger, lighter-coloured Chinese-type bitter melon (preferred in Southeast Asia and East Asia). Packed in ventilated cartons at 3 or 5 kg, despatched within 18 hours of harvest.

Indian & Chinese Type Air Freight Year-Round 3 / 5 kg Carton

Fresh Green Chilli

India is the world's largest producer of chillies, and Gujarat grows several commercially important fresh-market varieties. We export primarily the long, slender Jwala and the milder Bullet types to UK, UAE, Singapore, and Canada. MRL compliance for the EU and UK markets is our foremost priority — all consignments are pre-shipment tested at NABL labs. Packed in 5 kg perforated polybags or 5 kg ventilated cartons, despatched by air-freight or sea within strict cold-chain protocols.

Jwala & Bullet EU MRL Tested Year-Round 5 kg Carton

Drumstick — Moringa

Moringa oleifera — the drumstick tree — is rapidly becoming one of the world's most significant functional food ingredients. Fresh drumstick pods from Gujarat are exported to the UK, Canada, Australia, and across the Gulf, supplying South Indian restaurant trade and health-conscious consumers. Pods are harvested at 30–45 cm, when flesh is firm and seeds are small. Packed in perforated 5 kg cartons and dispatched air-freight within 12 hours of harvest to preserve maximum nutrient density.

30–45 cm Pod Superfood Oct – Mar Air Freight

Trade Specifications

Export Specifications & Terms

Our trade specifications are designed for maximum flexibility. Contact our export team to discuss custom grading, labelling, and scheduling options.

Minimum Order Onions: 1 × 20ft Container (~22–24 MT net). Perishables (Okra, Karela, Chilli, Drumstick): 200 kg minimum for air-freight consolidated; 5 MT for sea-cooled FCL. Trial shipments welcomed.
Packaging Onions: 20 kg or 25 kg jute / PP net sacks; Okra & Bitter Gourd: 3 kg or 5 kg ventilated cartons; Chilli: 5 kg cartons or 5 kg polybags; Drumstick: 5 kg perforated cartons. Private label on request.
Grading Onions: 35–45 mm, 45–55 mm, 55–65 mm, 65–75 mm diameter grades. Okra: 5–7 cm, 6–9 cm pod length. Drumstick: 30–40 cm, 40–50 cm length. Custom grading available.
Temperature & Shelf Life Onions: ambient or 2–4°C (40–60 days shelf life). Okra: 7–10°C (10–14 days). Bitter Gourd: 10–12°C (14–21 days). Chilli: 7–10°C (14 days). Drumstick: 10°C (10–12 days).

Payment Terms LC at sight · TT in advance for new accounts · Net 30 against invoice for approved annual-programme buyers.
Our Process

Field to Container —
The Zaffra Vegetable Chain

Precision at every step — from field-level agronomy support to real-time logistics tracking — ensures the quality your customers expect arrives reliably and on time.

01

Field Inspection

Our in-house agronomists conduct pre-harvest field inspections 5–7 days before picking. Maturity parameters — bulb size, pod length, skin condition, Brix, and firmness — are assessed and crops are cleared for harvest only when they meet our export grade standard.

02

Grading & Sorting

Produce is brought to our pack-house and passed through mechanical graders (onions) or hand-graded by trained sorters (okra, chilli, drumstick). Double-check sorting removes any below-grade or damaged units before packing.

03

Pack & Label

Graded produce is packed into buyer-specified formats with correct net weight, product label, country of origin, and lot traceability codes. APEDA-approved packaging materials are used for all export-certified consignments. MRL samples are drawn from each lot at this stage.

04

Cold Store & Despatch

Perishable vegetables enter our 400 MT cold-store facility at prescribed temperatures immediately after packing. Pre-shipment inspection is conducted by APEDA-approved inspectors, and phytosanitary certificates are issued before departure. Containers are sealed and temperature-logged for the voyage.
